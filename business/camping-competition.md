# Camping Werewolf Tournament

## The Idea

Approach camping animation teams and offer the app for free to let them organise a werewolf tournament as an evening activity. With 30 players you could run 3 parallel sessions simultaneously. The tournament can be a single evening or span multiple evenings over a holiday week, with players qualifying for a championship final.

**Example elimination format**
- Round 1: 4 groups of ~8 players play simultaneously
- Round 2: winners (and top scorers) from each group advance
- Championship evening: 1–2 final games to crown an overall winner

---

## Why Campings Are a Great Fit

- Animation teams are paid to fill evenings with organised group activities — you're solving their problem, not asking for a favour
- Large mixed groups of all ages in a relaxed social setting are ideal for this game
- A tournament gives the whole camp a shared storyline over multiple days, which is much stickier than a one-off game
- A summer werewolf tournament is the kind of thing people talk about the next day — free word-of-mouth among holiday-makers

---

## Strategic Value

- **Free pilot channel**: offer it free to campings; they become a distribution network that puts the app in front of thousands of players per summer
- **Home conversion**: players who experience it on holiday are natural premium subscribers — they want to replicate it with friends back home
- **Real-world validation**: immediately tests multi-session scoring and tournament features in a live setting, informing what to build next
- **Seasonality**: primarily a summer channel (June–August), high impact but time-boxed

---

## Potential Targets

- Large Dutch holiday parks: Center Parcs, Landal GreenParks, Roompot, Molecaten
- Any camping with a structured animation programme
- Contact: the animation coordinator or entertainment manager

---

## Things to Resolve First

- **Internet reliability**: campings often have patchy WiFi. The app needs to degrade gracefully, or offline resilience needs to be considered.
- **Tournament bracket mode**: running parallel sessions and combining winners requires a feature not yet built. For now this would be managed manually by the animator (they track which group won and set up the next game).
- **Scoring across sessions**: the persistent multi-game scoring feature (see [feature-roadmap.md](feature-roadmap.md)) is a prerequisite for the qualification-over-multiple-evenings format.

---

## The Championship Angle

If this works at one or two campings, a logical next step is a cross-camping championship — for example, "Dutch Camping Werewolf Champion 2027". Each participating camping holds a local tournament; winners advance to a national final evening. This is absurd enough to be memorable and real enough to be a genuine PR story.
