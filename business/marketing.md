# Werewolves App — Marketing

---

## Social Media Presence

### Platforms to be on
- **Twitter/X** — still where a lot of gaming conversation happens
- **Bluesky** — growing fast, skews toward tech/indie developers; good for early adopters
- **Instagram** — visual storytelling, short clips, tournament highlights
- **TikTok** — highest organic reach potential right now (see *Video Content* below)
- **Reddit** — not a posting platform but essential for community engagement (see below)

### Cross-platform posting
Rather than posting manually to each platform, use a scheduling and reposting tool:
- **Buffer** or **Hootsuite** — write once, publish everywhere, schedule ahead
- **Typefully** — good for threads and long-form posts
- With AI assistance, one piece of content can be reformatted automatically per platform tone (casual for TikTok, more detailed for Bluesky, visual-first for Instagram)

### AI-assisted content generation
- Use an LLM to draft posts, newsletters, and caption variations
- Feed it game summaries or funny moments → it generates post copy
- Generate a weekly/monthly newsletter summarising things like: new features, a featured tournament result, a "werewolf tip of the week"
- Keep a content calendar so posts go out consistently even when you're building features

## Social Listening & Engagement

The idea of scraping posts about werewolf games and engaging with them is legitimate and used by many indie products. The key is to do it authentically.

**What to monitor**
- Keywords: "werewolf game", "werewolf tonight", "played werewolf", "mafia game", "social deduction"
- Platforms: Twitter/X, Bluesky, Reddit, TikTok comments

**What to do with it**
- Reply with something genuinely useful or funny — not a spam mention of the app
- Retweet or boost posts where people are excited about werewolf games
- The bio does the work: anyone curious enough to click your profile sees what the app is

**Tools**
- Twitter/X Advanced Search or the API (rate-limited on free tier)
- **Mention.com** or **Brand24** for broader monitoring across platforms
- A simple automated script can flag posts for manual review before any reply goes out — important to keep replies human-feeling

**Important caveat**: fully automated replies are risky (can easily come across as spammy or tone-deaf). A human-in-the-loop model works better: AI surfaces candidates, you approve and maybe tweak before sending.

## Video Content

This is probably the highest-leverage channel given your game's nature — it's inherently visual, dramatic, and social.

**What makes great clips**
- A player getting caught out as a werewolf — the reveal moment
- A tense voting round where the group is split
- A werewolf staying completely calm under interrogation
- The Seer quietly steering the group without revealing themselves
- Someone confidently voting out the wrong person

**Short-form (TikTok / Instagram Reels / YouTube Shorts)**
- 15–60 second clips, heavily edited, with text overlays and reaction captions
- These can go viral without any following — the algorithm rewards content, not follower count
- A single good clip from a real game could bring thousands of new potential players

**How to get footage**
- Film your own test sessions
- Incentivise players at tournaments to share clips (offer a free premium month, feature them on the account)
- Run a "best clip of the month" contest — user-generated content (UGC) is free and more trusted than brand content

**Longer form (YouTube)**
- Full game walkthroughs can build a more engaged audience
- "Can you spot the werewolf?" interactive videos where viewers guess before the reveal

## Community Building

**Discord server**
- Create a server early, before you have many users
- People who find the app can join, give feedback, and feel like insiders
- Run exclusive tournaments for Discord members to reward early adopters
- The community becomes a natural source of beta testers

**Reddit**
- Don't create a subreddit immediately (empty subreddits look bad) — engage in existing ones first
- Relevant communities: r/boardgames, r/mafia (the game), r/socialdeduction, r/indiegaming
- Share genuinely useful posts (e.g., "How to run a werewolf tournament for 30+ people") — mention the app naturally, don't spam

## Tournaments as Marketing

Expanding on the camping idea: organising tournaments is not just a B2B channel, it's a marketing engine.

**Why this works**
- Every player at a tournament is a potential subscriber
- Tournaments generate content (clips, scores, drama) that feeds social media
- Winners become natural advocates — people who win at something tend to talk about it
- You don't need to monetise the tournaments themselves; they pay for themselves in user acquisition

**Where to run tournaments beyond campings**
- Board game cafés
- Universities / student associations (Werewolf is already popular in this demographic)
- Youth clubs and scouting groups
- Corporate team-building events (see [monetization-ideas.md](monetization-ideas.md))
- Gaming conventions (Spiel in Essen, Dutch Comic Con, etc.)

**Low-effort version**: provide a "run your own tournament" kit — a PDF guide + the app — so others can organise without your presence. This scales without requiring your time.

## Press & Influencers

**Board game / party game press**
- Board game blogs and YouTube channels (e.g., Shut Up & Sit Down, No Pun Included)
- A well-timed review from one of these can reach tens of thousands of exactly the right audience

**Tech / indie press**
- Product Hunt launch — especially good for getting early adopters and developer interest
- Indie game newsletters (e.g., Indie Game Business, Simon Carless's Game Discoverability Now)

**Local press**
- A story about a Dutch indie developer building an app that's now being used at campings across the Netherlands is a genuine human-interest story
- This is often easier to get than national gaming press and reaches a different audience

## SEO & Content Marketing

- Write articles targeting search terms people actually use: "how to play werewolf with a large group", "werewolf game rules", "online werewolf moderator app"
- These rank in Google and bring in organic traffic for years with no ongoing cost
- A simple blog on the website is enough to start

---

## Email List

- Capture emails early — from the app, the website, tournaments
- An email list is an asset you own, unlike social media followers (platforms can change algorithms or shut down)
- Even a small list of 500 engaged players is very valuable for launches and feature announcements

---

## What to Prioritise First

Given limited time, the highest-leverage starting points are:

1. **TikTok / Reels** — highest organic reach, lowest cost, your content is naturally dramatic
2. **Social listening + authentic engagement** — zero cost, builds presence gradually
3. **One tournament** — generates content, validates the concept, creates real player stories to tell
4. **Email capture** — start collecting from day one, even before you have anything to send

Everything else can come later once there's momentum.
